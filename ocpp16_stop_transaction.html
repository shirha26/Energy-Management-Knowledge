🔍 What is Stop Transaction?

▼
🛑

Definition

Stop Transaction is like "checking out of a hotel" - it's the official message that ends a charging session and provides a complete summary of what happened during the charging period.

📊

Final Summary

Contains the final meter reading, total energy consumed, any transaction data collected during the session, and the reason why charging ended.

💰

Billing Trigger

This message triggers the final billing calculation. Like getting your restaurant bill - it shows exactly what you consumed and what you owe.

🔄

Session Closure

Officially closes the transaction in all systems, releases the connector for the next user, and archives all session data for records.

🔄 Complete Stop Transaction Process

▼

1. Charging Ends

Car finishes charging or user stops it

2. Cable Disconnected

User unplugs cable from vehicle

3. Final Readings

System captures final meter values

4. Stop Message

Station sends transaction summary

5. Billing & Cleanup

Final bill calculated, connector released

1Charging Completion

What happens: The charging process reaches its natural end point or is deliberately stopped by the user.

🔋 Real-World Example:

Like your phone reaching 100% battery and stopping charging automatically, or you deciding to unplug it early because you need to leave.

Common Ways Charging Ends:

• Battery Full: Vehicle reaches target charge level (most common)

• User Stops: User decides to end charging early

• Time Limit: Maximum charging time reached

• Scheduled End: Pre-programmed stop time arrived

• Emergency Stop: Safety system intervention

2Physical Disconnection

What happens: The user physically removes the charging cable from their vehicle.

🔌 Real-World Example:

Like unplugging your laptop charger when you're ready to go. The charging station detects that the connection has been broken and knows the session is ending.

System Detections:

• Connection Lost: No more electrical connection to vehicle

• Safety Check: Ensures all power has stopped flowing

• Cable Status: Confirms cable is properly returned/secured

• Connector Release: Unlocks connector from vehicle if needed

3Final Meter Reading

What happens: The charging station captures the final energy meter reading and calculates total consumption.

📊 Real-World Example:

Like a taxi driver checking the final fare on the meter when you reach your destination. The system records exactly how much energy was delivered during the entire trip.

Data Captured:

• Final kWh Reading: Total energy delivered to vehicle

• Session Duration: Total time from start to stop

• Peak Power: Highest power level during session

• Average Efficiency: Overall charging performance

• Any Interruptions: Pauses or issues during charging

4Stop Transaction Message

What happens: The charging station sends a comprehensive "Stop Transaction" message to the management system with all session details.

📨 Real-World Example:

Like a hotel sending your final bill to your credit card company - it includes all the details: what room you stayed in, how long, what you consumed, and the total charges.

📄 Example: Stop Transaction JSON Message

{
  "messageType": "StopTransaction",
  "transactionId": 98765,
  "idTag": "RFID12345678",
  "timestamp": "2025-08-07T16:45:00.000Z",
  "meterStop": 24750,
  "reason": "EVDisconnected",
  "transactionData": [
    {
      "timestamp": "2025-08-07T16:45:00.000Z",
      "sampledValue": [
        {
          "value": "24.75",
          "context": "Transaction.End",
          "measurand": "Energy.Active.Import.Register",
          "unit": "kWh"
        }
      ]
    }
  ]
}

What this means: "Transaction #98765 ended at 4:45 PM on August 7th when the user (RFID12345678) disconnected their car. Final meter reading: 24.75 kWh total energy delivered."

5Billing & System Cleanup

What happens: The management system processes the final bill, charges the customer, and prepares the charging station for the next user.

💳 Real-World Example:

Like checking out of a hotel - they charge your credit card, give you a receipt, clean the room, and make it available for the next guest.

Final Steps:

• Calculate Final Bill: Energy cost + any time-based fees

• Process Payment: Charge customer's payment method

• Send Receipt: Email or app notification with session details

• Update Records: Store transaction data for reporting

• Reset Connector: Make charging port available for next user

• Status Update: Change connector status to "Available"

📄 Example: Stop Transaction Response

{
  "messageType": "StopTransactionResponse",
  "idTagInfo": {
    "status": "Accepted"
  }
}

What this means: "Management system confirms: 'Transaction successfully ended and processed. Thank you!'"

❓ Stop Reasons Explained

▼

🔌 EVDisconnected

What it means: The user unplugged the charging cable from their car.

Real-world: Most common and normal way charging ends. Like unplugging your phone charger when you're ready to go.

👤 Local

What it means: Someone physically pressed the stop button on the charging station.

Real-world: User decided to end charging early by pressing the stop button on the charger, similar to pressing "stop" on a gas pump.

📱 Remote

What it means: The management system or user remotely commanded the session to stop.

Real-world: User used their mobile app to stop charging, or the management system stopped it due to grid demands or maintenance needs.

🔋 PowerLoss

What it means: The charging station lost electrical power from the grid.

Real-world: Like a power outage at your house - the charger can't continue because there's no electricity to provide.

⏱️ ReaderTimeout

What it means: The card reader or authorization system stopped responding.

Real-world: Like when a credit card reader freezes at a store - the system can't verify the user anymore so it stops for safety.

🚨 EmergencyStop

What it means: Someone hit the emergency stop button or a safety system activated.

Real-world: Like pulling the emergency brake - immediate shutdown for safety reasons, such as equipment malfunction or dangerous situation.

🔒 DeAuthorized

What it means: The user's authorization was revoked during the charging session.

Real-world: Like having your credit card declined mid-transaction - perhaps the account was suspended or payment method expired during charging.

🔧 Other

What it means: The session ended for a reason not covered by the standard categories.

Real-world: Catch-all category for unusual situations - could be software updates, maintenance requirements, or other operational needs.

💡 What Happens to Transaction Data?

▼

📊 Data Archival

All transaction data is permanently stored for billing, reporting, and compliance purposes.

🗃️ What Gets Saved:

• Complete energy consumption records
• All meter readings during the session
• User identification and authorization
• Start and stop times with precise timestamps
• Any errors or issues that occurred
• Payment and billing information

💰 Billing Processing

The final transaction data triggers the billing system to calculate charges and process payment.

💳 Billing Components:

• Energy Charges: Cost per kWh consumed
• Time Charges: Connection fees or time-based rates
• Service Fees: Network access or processing fees
• Tax Calculations: Local tax rates applied
• Discounts/Credits: Membership benefits or promotions

📈 Analytics & Reporting

Transaction data feeds into analytics systems for operational insights and business intelligence.

📊 Analytics Uses:

• Usage Patterns: Peak times and popular locations
• Customer Behavior: Charging preferences and frequency
• Equipment Performance: Charger reliability and efficiency
• Revenue Analysis: Financial performance and trends
• Grid Impact: Energy demand patterns

📚 Summary

▼

Key Takeaway: Stop Transaction is like the final checkout process that officially ends a charging session. It captures all the essential data needed for billing, provides a complete record of the session, and ensures the charging station is properly reset for the next user.

✅

What We Learned

• Stop Transaction formally ends charging sessions
• Contains final billing data and session summary
• Different stop reasons indicate why charging ended
• Triggers billing, archival, and system cleanup

🎯

Real-World Impact

• Ensures accurate customer billing
• Provides audit trail for transactions
• Enables business analytics and insights
• Maintains system efficiency and availability
